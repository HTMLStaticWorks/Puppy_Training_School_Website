5 Mistakes to Avoid When Potty Training Your Puppy

John Doe
Master Trainer • Oct 12, 2026 • 5 min read

Bringing a new puppy home is an exciting experience, but the joy is often offset by the stress of potty training. Accidents are inevitable, but how you react to them and the routines you establish dictate how quickly your dog will learn.

1. Waiting for the Puppy to Ask

Puppies do not inherently know how to ask to go outside. If you wait for them to bark at the door, you will be mopping up an accident. You must take your puppy out on a strict schedule.

2. Punishing Accidents After the Fact

If you find a puddle in the living room and yell at your dog 10 minutes later, they will not associate your anger with the puddle. They will only learn to fear you. If you don't catch them in the act, you simply clean it up.

"Consistency is the currency of puppy training. Without it, your dog simply guesses what you want."

3. Returning Inside Too Quickly

Many owners bring the puppy back inside the exact second they finish peeing. The dog learns that peeing ends the fun outside time, so they hold it to prolong the walk.

Remember, patience and a pocket full of high-value treats will solve almost any potty training issue!
