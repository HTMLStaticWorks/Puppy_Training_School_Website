Mastering Loose Leash Walking

Mike Chen
Master Trainer â€˘ Sep 20, 2026 â€˘ 5 min read

Walking should be a joy, not a tug-of-war. Many puppies pull because they are excited to get to the next smell. Here is how to slow them down.

The 'Be a Tree' Method

As soon as the leash goes taut, stop walking. Don't pull back—just wait. When the puppy looks back at you or the leash slackens, reward them and continue forward.

Indoor Practice

The best place to learn leash walking is in your hallway where there are zero distractions. Master it there before trying the great outdoors.
